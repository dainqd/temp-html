Talkshow “books read me”: Write to connect” – My own books”

Do you know? Writing our own books is to put what we know, what we understand, what we own or what we have experienced to help others also know, understand and feel. Okay. There are people, they don't keep the stories they see, they hear only for themselves. They write, write books, pen to pages and bring them to us, to life.

In the collection of short stories "Half Filled the World" - t he book was compiled by 19 best authors who entered the final round of the short story contest "Half Filled the World" (with the same name) organized by Culture Publishing House. – Ho Chi Minh City Arts and C ulture is organized with the sponsorship of a project under the Liberatupreis Prize – Frankfurt 2018. One of the 19 stories in that collection of short stories is “The Road to Dreams” by author Huynh Lam Minh Tri – Mr. F-school student, same age as us. Tri writes about the lives of modern Vietnamese women who come out with countless wounds patched by the bumps of life. These stories recreate the images of women around us. Maybe they are relatives or strangers caught in the middle of social prejudices, because of the binding frameworks that people quickly put on them, whether they agree or not.

In short, all those books, those stories, bring us emotions, love and beautiful messages about life. To better understand, we cordially invite you to the Talkshow "Book Read Me 03: Writing To Connect", to meet the two writers of the above two books, lecturer Ho Yen Thuc and student Huynh Lam Minh Tri . What complementary messages, knowledge and skills will they bring to us in the journey of "writing the pen" to communicate? Look forward to see!
